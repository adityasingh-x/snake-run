# Gameplay

## Food Variants

### Gold Food

Description:

Rare food worth significantly more points than normal food.

Possible Rules:

- Worth 3x normal score
- Despawns after a short duration
- Distinct visual appearance

Why:

Creates risk/reward decisions and encourages movement across the board.

Status:

Backlog

---

### Poison Food

Description:

Food that harms the player rather than helping.

Possible Rules:

- Reduces score
- Shrinks snake length
- Temporarily increases speed

Why:

Introduces decision-making beyond simply eating everything.

Status:

Backlog

---

### Speed Food

Description:

Food that temporarily modifies game speed.

Possible Rules:

- Speed boost
- Slow-motion effect
- Limited duration

Why:

Creates situational decisions and gameplay variety.

Status:

Backlog

---

### Magnet Food

Description:

Temporary attraction effect that pulls nearby food toward the snake.

Why:

Creates powerful but time-limited gameplay moments.

Status:

Backlog

Backlog

---

### Portal Levels

Description:

Paired portal tiles teleport the snake.

Why:

Creates unique navigation puzzles.

Status:

Backlog

---

### Maze Levels

Description:

Hand-designed obstacle layouts instead of random obstacle placement.

Why:

Creates memorable levels and more meaningful progression.

Problem Solved:

Current levels feel similar because obstacle placement is random.

Status:

Backlog

---

### Moving Obstacles

Description:

Obstacles periodically change position or move along paths.

Why:

Introduces dynamic hazards.

Status:

Backlog

---

### Distinct Level Themes

Description:

Each level introduces a unique gameplay concept.

Examples:

- Classic
- Maze
- Portal
- Wrap-around
- High-speed
- Dense obstacles

Why:

Players should be able to remember specific levels.

Problem Solved:

Backlog
